1. What is Programathon?
Programathon is a tournament in which computer science students, professionals and those who have programming skills can demonstrate their abilities in a programming challenge set to be resolved in a stablished timeframe. For this edition, Programathon is a pro-education challenge.
2. When is the event going to take place?
Programathon will take place on September, 20th and 21st, 2014.
3. Where is the event going to take place?
Programathon will be held in the Auditorio Jean Piaget, Fundación Omar Dengo. This is from Casa Matute Gomez 300meters east, 75 meters South, Barrio Francisco Peralta, Calle 25A, Avenida 10 y 10B.
4. What will be the length of this event?
Approximately 36 hours. The tournament will start on Saturday, September 20th at 7:00 am and finishes on Sunday, September 21st at 5:00pm.
5. What about food, rest areas, snacks:
Don't worry, everything is included
6. Who organizes this event? Who is the sponsor?
Programathon is organized and sponsored by Fiserv Costa Rica.
7. Will teams have to start from scratch?
Before the event starts, one participant from each team needs to join an induction session in which they will receive informative material, important information related to the event and the regulations. This induction session will be held on September 18th, 2014. On Saturday, September 20th, once the event starts, every team will receive the solution's requirements document, they will have then an established timeframe to get organized and start programming a solution to the challenged given. This solution will be developed from scratch, with the resources available and under the lineaments stated on the regulations. The tournament is planned in a way that the participants will be delivering functionalities throughout Saturday and Sunday. These deliverables will be graded depending on the assessment criteria established for the event.
8. To whom is Programathon addressed to?
Programathon is addressed to students and professionals in computer science, and anyone else who has programming skills.
9. Participating on this event will have a cost?
The participation on this event will have no cost; it is only required to register at programathon.cr.
10. What will be the process for picking out the teams?
The teams will have to register on programathon.cr, there will be a basic programming evaluation in the language that needs to be used for the solution. The capacity will remain open until we reach a maximum of 200 participants.
11. Is this a public event?
Yes
12. Who will be the beneficiary of the tool developed?
Programathon selected Fundación Omar Dengo (FOD) to be the administrator and developer of the solutions that this event generates. FOD is a nonprofit organization; its main objective is to develop skills with the use of innovative educational proposals on new technologies. Since 1987, Fundación Omar Dengo executes national projects related to human development, educational innovation and introduction of new technologies to a broad group of population in the country that in other circumstances would not be able to access technology education due to its cost.
FOD will be working internally in the evaluation and viability of the solution in order to adapt it and propose it in the pilot projects in benefit of preschool, students from the integrated class (I and II cycle) and students from special education (II cycle).
13. From all the software developed during the event, how many of the solutions delivered by the participants will be used?
The winning solution will be the one used for this pilot project. It is important to highlight the fact that FOD can reutilized any code from others teams.
14. Are the participants going to have copy rights over the developed tool?
No, all rights will be granted to FOD, and they will be used under FOD discretion.
15. What will be the winner prices?
First place - $3,000 – Second Place - $2,000 – Third Place $1,000.
